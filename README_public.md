## What is EmiTrack?

EmiTrack is a national digital platform for **measuring, benchmarking, and making
decisions on** greenhouse-gas emissions from Ghana's upstream oil & gas operations.

It takes the routine production data that regulators already collect — monthly oil,
gas, flare, fuel and export volumes — and turns it into a clear, defensible picture
of where methane and CO₂ come from, how Ghana compares to the rest of the world, and
what the cheapest, highest-impact ways to cut emissions are.

No new sensors, no specialist training, and no data you don't already have.

---

## What you can do with it

🔎 **See where emissions come from.** Each facility's methane is broken down across
twelve process sources, with uncertainty bounds, so you can see exactly where to act.

🌍 **Benchmark against the world.** Carbon intensity is measured on a saleable-energy
basis and set against Angola, the US Gulf of Mexico and the global crude average.

🗺️ **Know what to measure next.** A ranking shows the single highest-value measurement
for each facility — a costed basis for a national monitoring programme.

💡 **Find the cheapest wins.** The platform identifies the lowest-cost, highest-impact
way to cut emissions (repairing flare combustion efficiency, at ≈US$12 per tonne CO₂e).

🧪 **Test "what-if" scenarios live.** The **Scenario Lab** lets you move sliders for
flaring, flare efficiency and production and watch carbon intensity, methane and the
source mix update instantly — a *Simple* mode for quick answers and an *Advanced* mode
for full uncertainty.

---

## How to use it

1. Open the site — you'll land on the **home page**.
2. Click **Explore the dashboard** to browse the ten analytical views, or
   **Launch Scenario Lab** to start experimenting straight away.
3. Inside the dashboard, use the left-hand menu to move between views. In the
   **Scenario Lab**, pick a field (or compare all three), choose what to look at,
   and move the sliders — everything recalculates in real time.

> The Scenario Lab always starts from the validated baseline. Anything you change
> is a "what-if" only — it never alters the official published figures.

Works in any modern web browser (Chrome, Edge, Safari, Firefox) on desktop or laptop.
An internet connection is needed so the charts and fonts load correctly.

## The headline findings

| Result | What it means |
|--------|----------------|
| **1.9 – 2.6** g CO₂e MJ⁻¹ | Jubilee and OCTP/SGN carbon intensity — **below** Angola (3.6), the US Gulf of Mexico (~7) and the global crude average (10.3). |
| **60 – 80%** | Share of each facility's methane that comes from **flaring** — one dominant source that is also the cheapest to fix and most valuable to measure. |
| **≈82 kt** | Cumulative fugitive & vented methane, 2018–Q1 2026 (≈2.5 Mt CO₂e) across three deepwater FPSOs. |
| **≈78 kt** | Methane avoidable to 2040 under disciplined gas management — a concrete low-carbon growth story. |

Baseline period: **2018 – Q1 2026**, covering 27 field-years across Jubilee, TEN and
OCTP/SGN. Methane is converted to CO₂-equivalent using GWP₁₀₀ = 29.8.

## Who built it

EmiTrack was developed by the **Net-Zero Carbon Emission Lab (NCEL)** at the College
of Engineering, **Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (KNUST)**, and
prepared for the **Petroleum Commission of Ghana**.

The platform can be updated with new production returns at any time, and the same
framework transfers to any basin that keeps routine regulator data.
