The platform

Everything the Commission needs, in one interface

Ten analytical views and a live Scenario Lab in a single interface — requiring no technical training, and resting entirely on data the Commission already holds.

Source-resolved inventory

Each facility's methane split across twelve process sources with uncertainty bounds — showing where emissions originate and where to act.

Global benchmarking

Carbon intensity on a saleable-energy basis, set against airborne Angola, the US Gulf of Mexico and the global crude average.

Measurement roadmap

A value-of-information ranking that names the single highest-value measurement for each facility — a costed basis for national monitoring.

Least-cost abatement

Flare combustion-efficiency repair identified as the lowest-cost, highest-impact lever at ≈US$12 per tonne CO₂e.

Live Scenario Lab

Choose a field and adjust flaring, flare efficiency and production; carbon intensity, methane and the source mix update instantly — in a Simple mode for quick answers or an Advanced mode for full uncertainty.

Updatable & transferable

Load new production returns to recompute in-browser. The framework transfers to any basin holding routine regulator data.

How it works

From regulator data to decision

The platform rests on data the Commission already holds — no new instrumentation is required to begin.

Metered production in

Monthly field-aggregated returns — oil, gas, flare, fuel and export volumes — the auditable record the Commission already receives.

Physics + uncertainty

A validated twelve-source operator maps activity to methane and intensity, with a 40,000-draw Monte-Carlo propagating every uncertainty.

Intelligence out

Benchmarked intensity, a ranked measurement roadmap, least-cost abatement and forward scenarios — all interactive and re-runnable.
